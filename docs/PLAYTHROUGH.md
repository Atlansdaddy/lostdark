# wAIver — A Full Playthrough (intro → endgame+)

> A representative single-player run, start to finish, showing how the systems and the story compound. It doubles as a design stress-test: if the loop reads as *fun and meaningful* across this whole arc, the game holds. (Co-op notes in *italics*.) Biome order: **The Reek → The Bite → The Drown → The Sear → The Glare → The Fade → The Nothing.** (v1 ships Reek·Bite·Drown·Sear·Nothing.)

---

## 0. Awakening — *The Reek* (the intro, ~10–15 min)
You come to as a dim, barely-lit orb in a warm bioluminescent hollow. No menu of verbs, no prompt — just a soft glow ahead. Drifting toward it teaches you to **move** (hover-glide); a first instinctive **pulse** blooms the cavern into view for a breath, then fades. You learn to *see* by wanting to see.

A second light waits ahead — smaller, warmer, guttering: **the Ember.** It doesn't speak; it *chimes*, and leans the way it wants you to go. It leads you to a cluster of **glowcaps**; you **gather** glow-spores and **place your first ward** — and the hollow brightens *around you and stays bright*. Light is suddenly something you *make and own*.

Then the flora dims. A low resonance, a tremor — heard before seen — and the **first Dark Tide** surges in. It's scary and new but survivable: you pull into your ward's light, a few **Snuffers** clinging and dimming your glow, and the ward *holds*. In the trembling dark you glimpse, far off, an extinguished beacon on a spire — a whisper that *someone held the light here before, and failed*. The tide recedes; the world is a shade darker for it; and you understand the loop because you just lived it. The Ember chimes, softer, and drifts onward.

## 1. The Reek — learning to hold (first bastion)
Now the sandbox opens. You range out under the reeking glow-mist, **pulsing** to read the fog, **gathering** glowcaps and spore-sacs, and raising your first real **bastion** — a lit core with a ward field. **Chokers** fog your sight until you learn the **pressure-pulse** parts vapor; a lumbering **Bloat** teaches you to read a tell and kill at range. Tides come again, a little worse each time; your walls (and your first instinct toward *separation* — a second ward so one breach doesn't cost everything) answer them.

The chapter's exam is the **Tender** — a vast, half-plant apex you hear breathing in the canopy-dark, the great glow-life that once lit this place, now hollow but still *trying to grow its children*. You beat it by everything the Reek taught: watch the mist brighten (the tell), part its exhale, flare through its draw. It doesn't die screaming — it dims, breathes one last cloud of spores that catch your light and glow, and is gone. The Reek's first, tender goodbye. *(In co-op, a second Spark holds the far ward while you press the tell — you feel, immediately, why light shared is light that lasts.)*

## 2. The Bite — the first real pressure
The Ember leads you up out of the warm mist into blue-white stillness. Here **cold pressure** is a survival clock: away from warmth your Lumen *dims and your orb visibly shivers* — the first time the environment itself is trying to kill you. You learn **ice** at a frozen spring, freezing meltwater into brittle bridges and shattering them behind you. Combat is a race against your own cooling: **The Still** (frozen figures that lunge, re-frozen solid by your flare into stepping-stones), **Chatter** (swarming ice-teeth), **The Hush** (a creeping frost-front that snuffs your outer lamps — teaching you to *consolidate* warmth, not spread it thin).

The apex, **The Kept**, is a Keeper frozen curled around a stolen coal it would not share. You reach it across ice you freeze yourself, crack the shell over the ember — and the hoarded warmth floods out, re-lighting the whole gallery and thawing the reaching dead into stillness. The theme lands without a word: *hoarding the light froze everyone reaching for it.* You leave with the tier-2 material the next biome's wards will need.

## 3. The Drown — depth, and the price of clinging
Down into drowned cathedral-gloom. This is the showpiece: your light **bends and pools** through the water, and you learn **water projection** — streaming reflective pools that ferry your glow around corners. But **pressure crushes an unlit descent and darkness drowns**, so you can only go as deep as your carried light and air hold. **Beckons** dangle false lights to lure you past your depth; **Hushers** drink the water's shimmer; **Undertows** haul you toward the drop.

The apex, **The Last Hour**, doesn't attack — she *keeps* you, a drowned refuser of the ending who dims the water a notch each time you accept her false dawn, bleeding your air out while the room *reads* as sunlit safety. You win by refusing her light and carrying your own down to her true face — *letting the day end.* The deep stops swallowing your beam. The Ember, brighter for a moment near the water's memory of light, chimes and sinks with you.

## 4. The Sear — light that burns
Fire as sun. Your orb runs **red-hot**, and for the first time light is a *hammer*, not a lamp — you learn **heat**: warm to melt, melt to **explode**, and the giddy **thermal chain reactions** that are the game's showcase physics. But mastery is restraint: overheat and *you* become the chain reaction. The echoes here are the burnt — **Overhot** Keeper-echoes that feed on your glow, **Flashscabs** that daisy-chain and detonate, **Smoulders** waiting to be lit. You fight by running *cold* as often as hot.

The apex, the **Hearth-That-Spreads**, has *no attacks, only hunger* — it pulls your heat inward toward overheat and seeds Flashscab chains; attack it hot and the room detonates ring by ring. You beat it by refusing to feed it: gutter your own light, walk cold sparks to sever the chains, and smother its chest-fire with *darkness*. It only ever wanted to keep one small fire alive; you do it the kindness of letting it go cold. *(Co-op shines here — one Spark baits the heat while another severs chains; a badly-timed blast wipes you both, which is the joke and the lesson.)*

## 5. The Glare — the copy that isn't the flame
A blinding hall of prisms — the Keepers' proudest work, where they tried to *keep* the light by copying it into a thousand facets. Now seeing is a puzzle: light **ricochets**, real and mirrored paths look identical, and the Dark hides *in the reflection*. You learn **light-mastery** — focusing your pulse to a beam, bending it through prisms, bouncing it around corners, singing resonance-crystals open. Crucially, counterplay often means **dimming down** to see the truth.

The apex, **The Flare**, is a reflection of *you* that mirrors every shot — match it and you only tie. You win by striking from an angle it can't copy, or shattering the mirror it stands in. *A reflection is not a fire.* The memory here is the saddest yet — a Keeper's voice: *"We will keep every last ray"* … then, later, *"I can no longer tell which light is the one I was keeping."* The Ember flickers hard, as if it remembers.

## 6. The Fade — the truth (story core)
A grey ash-dusk of dead beacons and fallen Keepers: their graveyard, and your lineage. You learn **electricity** from guttering Keeper-machines — draw, carry, release charge; a charged puddle kills Spark and enemy alike. The **Revenants** are the Keepers themselves, hollow, still *guarding hoarded light* — they flinch from your brightness like a memory of who they were, and you can free them rather than fight them.

Climb the **Bastion Spire** and the truth is wordless: the great city's **walls face inward.** Every gate sealed against the *next Keeper over*. They didn't fall to the Dark — they fell **alone**, each hoarding a light that ended in their hands. The memory-blooms here are the richest in the game — a room of Keepers whispering *hold on*; and two at a failing beacon, one gently prying the other's fingers open — *let it go* — and for a heartbeat the light doesn't die, it *spreads*. The apex, the **Warden of the Inward Wall**, is the one who clung hardest; you beat it not by out-burning it but by turning its walls *outward*, releasing the light it kept. It doesn't fall — it *sets the light down*, the thing it could not do alone, and lets you carry it on. You now know what you are, and what you must not become.

## 7. The Nothing — transcendence (endgame)
The Ember leads you to the bottom of the world: near-total black, no ambient light at all — *you are the only light that exists here.* This is **the wound**, where the First Light reached the end of a life and the Keepers clung hardest and the Dark rushed in first. Every tide in the world began here. The enemies are everything, remixed: **Breachers** that have learned your last three bastions, **Everythings** wearing all their deaths at once, and **Snuffkin** — the very first enemy you met, returned as the last. The tides are the worst in the game — the whole ocean of the Dark at once.

You reach the last beacon, and the **Ember guts out** — spends its final warmth to bring you here, and goes dark. Your own light begins to fail. This is the low; the player believes it ends. It doesn't. You touch the Endbloom and see the final, gentle truth — the First Light simply *closing its eyes*, the way all lights end; the tragedy was never the ending, only the clinging — and you *let go.*

And then you **ignite** — not a weapon-pulse, a *creation* pulse. The loop flips: **each tide you hold becomes ground.** You stop defending and start *building* — you are the beacon now, laying lit, living world faster than the Maw can unmake it, varying faster than the Breachers learn, out-shining the Snuffkin. When your created ground meets from both horizons, the dark has nowhere left to be thickest. The tide breaks — and, for the first time in the world's history, **recedes.** *You didn't win the fight. You ended the reason for it.*

## 8. Endgame+ — the new world
Dawn is just the name for how far your light reached. What was the Dark's heart is now a lit, buildable frontier, and the tides that were catastrophe become a *heartbeat* you can weather. The endgame loop is **creation, not survival**: you expand the new world outward, raise great bastions where the Nothing was, and — *the ending's real payoff* — **other Sparks arrive.** Faint answering glimmers become players gathering to the world you made; *held light died with the Keepers, but shared light is a place people come home to.*

Post-game keeps the Escalation Loop alive in a hopeful key: deeper unexplored dark still exists to reclaim, the stubbed biomes and future content extend the frontier, and (roadmap) the toggled **PvPvE / base-raiding** layer turns your bastions into something to defend *from each other* as well as the dark. But the arc is complete: a single spark, born in a dying world, passed all the way through the end of a life, and became the beginning of the next.
